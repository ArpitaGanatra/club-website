Culture II

Analysis by Jonathan Morales

Although I didn't listen to the album in it's entirety, it was a dissapointment. After the rap group's recent work Culture I, I learned that the Migos have a very specific and generic style that they don't stray away from. Quavo and Offset, two of the group's members, released collaborative projects with other artist's where they were forced to adopt new styles. Those projects were successful because of the unique stylistic adjustements the artists made to fit their project. This album feels as though they were more comfortable than innovative. This project definitely has a few strong tracks but it's nothing we haven't heard before.

One Paragraph, Many Tears

Reddit Can Make You So Sad

"Albert?" She asks, and unseeing eyes wander over to the doorway. I tell her yes. The nurses tell me that it's better if I go along with it. It's better to make her happy than confuse and scare her with the truth. I go to her, I put flowers in the vase, I ask her about her day. She rambles, a little incoherently, about something that happened ten years ago. I smile and nod. She suddenly tells me that she wants to read my palm, and I give her my hand. I realize my mistake only afterwards. She stops, stiffens. A look of pure fury plays over her face. I try to ask her what's wrong and she starts screaming. She stands up, wobbling and keeling dangerously, and starts throwing things at me. Books, mostly, but a snowglobe hits the corner of her dresser and shatters. I'm not wearing the ring. Why am I not wearing the ring? Am I cheating on her? Do I not love her anymore? She's sobbing and I can't move. I don't know what to do. A nurse runs in and pulls me away, out of the room. A couple more attendants go into her room to calm her down. I sit down on a bench in the hallway and try to tell the nurse that this is wrong, I want to tell her the truth. The nurse just shakes her head and leaves me alone. I know she's right. The last three times now have ended in tears, because she can remember the husband who died ten years ago well enough to recognize every mistake I make but she can't remember that she has a grandson. I'm the only one who even bothers to visit anymore. After ten minutes of sitting on the bench I realize I left my phone in the room. I walk up to the door and open it hesitantly, afraid that she's still mad. I open my mouth to apologize. She turns towards me with a vacant, glassy stare and slight frown. I want to throw up. She was the smartest person in my life for 20 years. I visit her every day. "Albert?" She asks.
